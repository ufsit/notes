# Overview
* The C-Suite Panel brief is one part of the *Orange Team score* in the competition
* goal is to expand soft skills: public/professional speaking engagement on technical topics to technical and non-technical executives
* members of the C-Suite are usually focused on **business outcomes** rather than technical details
  * interested and understand strategies and business principles, rather than the technical parts

# Benefits of the C-Suite Panel
* Important to relay technical knowledge to a non-technical audience (impact, risk, consequences) who are interested in financial burden
* They want the "elevator pitch"
* Focus on
  * how your strategic ideas identifies business risk
  * improve the company's overall future position 
  * protect the company's information
  * address the monetary cost its their justifications
  * relay all the above in simple terms

*It's literally Shark Tank irl*

# Preparation
* Understand the Organization and Panel
  * what is the org's mission and/or core values
  * know recent relevant info: current performance, staff initiatives, challenges, tight budget
* Simplicity is Key
  * don't get caught up in the weeds
  * clear, concise, and direct
  * less is more
* Structure
  * set the message based on the goal you want to achieve and drive that goal
* Nonverbal communication
  * Tone of voice, posture, clothing, pitch, rate of speech
  * Be aware of these factors, use them in your favor
* Think Strategy
  * never present a problem without a solution
  * Strategy = long term planning
  * provide the big picture and future impact
  * explain how your solution impacts the business' trajectory
  * have financials, people, relatable material

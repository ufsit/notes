# Social Media Policy
### Purpose
* [Organization] recognizes the prevalence of social media in today's society, and the importance of the Internet and social media in promoting the company, its products, and services. This policy is intended to assist employees to carry on a respectful knowledgeable interaction with people on the Internet, as well as protect the privacy, confidentiality, and interests of the company and its current potential products, services, employees, and customers. This policy, and related guidelines, apply only to work-related social media issues and are not intended to infringe upon employees' personal online interactions, commentary, or social media usage. 
### Employee Use of Social Media
There is a significant difference in speaking on behalf of the company as an official representative and speaking about the company in a personal capacity. The following guidelines refer to those personal or unofficial online activities where employees might refer to [Organization]
* All employees are subject to the Code of Conduct in every public setting. In addition, other policies, including the Information Security Policy and Privacy Policy, govern employees' behavior with respect to the disclosure of non-public information and are applicable to employees' personal activities online. All company policies that apply to off-duty conduct also apply to social media activity, including but not limited to policies related to illegal harassment, code of business conduct, nondiscrimination, and protecting confidential and/or proprietary information
* Employees must never disclose non-public information about [Organization] or its customers or stakeholders, including the fact that certain individuals are customers or stakeholders. Employees should be aware that taking public positions online that tare counter to company interests could create a conflict of interest
* Employees may not share or post internal documents, videos, or photographs online without written authorization form the Marketing Officer or a senior manager
* The company's logo and trademarks may not be used without written permission from the Marketing Officer or a senior manager. This is to prevent he appearance that employees speak for or officially represent [Organization]. EMployees should not include company logos in their personal online signature
* Employees should not link their personal social media page to the company website, or post any company advertising or other company material on a social media site without written permission from the Marketing Officer or a senior manager
* If employees publish content online relevant to the company, they should use a disclaimer such as the following: "The postings on this site are my own and don't necessarily represent the positions, strategies, or opinions of [Organization]."
* Despite disclaimers, an employee's online interactions may result in members of the public forming opinion about the company and its employees, partners, and products. Employees should conduct themselves professionally and ensure that information given online is accurate. 
* Employees should not respond directly to online complaints or disparaging remarks about the company unless the employee has been authorized to speak on behalf of [Organization]
* If an employee comes across online comments made regarding the company, positive or negative, the employee should share this information with the Marketing IOfficer prior to posting.
* Employees may not post any company-related comment or picture involving a company employee, partner, or customer without the consent of that individual.
* Employees are responsible for all information they post on social media, including pictures, video, and text, and assume all legal liability for any posts that may be considered by others as defamatory, harassing, libelous, or creating a hostile work environment. [Organization] will not be liable for or provide a defense for any legal action that results from an employee's harmful, hateful, or disparaging remarks made online. 
* Online remarks that are made by an employee against the company or its employees, officers, or customers that are harmful, hateful, or disparaging could result in disciplinary action against the employee, including termination of employment. 
* Employees must respect all copyright and financial disclosure laws and must not post content that may be considered the intellectual property of another without permission from the intellectual property owner. Intellectual property is any intellectual product that has commercial value, including copyrighted property such as literary or artistic works, and ideational property, such as patents or business methods.
* [Organization] reserves the right to request that an employee temporarily confine online comments to topics unrelated to the company if management believes this is necessary or advisable to ensure compliance with securities regulations or other laws
* Employees may not use company equipment for non-work related activities without permission. Personal use of computers or other devices, including personal social media activities, should not interfere with an employee's duties at work. Employee Internet and email usage on company networks and devices is subject to monitoring at any time. 
* Employee's use of social media is not permitted if it:
  * Interferes with the employee's work
  * Is used to harass or discriminate against fellow employees or customers
  * Creates a hostile work environment
  * Divulges confidential information about the company or its customers
  * Harms the reputation of the company or its customers
### Blogging
[Organization] recognizes that blogs are a means of personal expression for its employees and, in general, supports their employees in using and creating personal weblogs. However, the following restrictions apply to all blogs created, operated, or commented on by any employee:
* Unless officially authorized in advance and in writing by senior management, no company resources will be used to sponsor or host any blogging site or software for personal use. 
* Unless officially authorized in advance and in writing by senior management, no mention of the company by name or identifiable reference will be made in any personal blog
* All comments made by an employee are the responsibility of that employee and should be identified as personal comments that are not to be attributed to the company, its officers, or employees
* All comments must adhere to the privacy and confidentiality policies of the company
* [Organization] will not be accountable for or provide defense for any lawsuits or legal action brought about as a result of any harmful, hateful, or otherwise disparaging remarks made on a personal blog
* Harmful, hateful, or otherwise disparaging remarks targeting specific employees of, officers of, or the company itself made by an employee on a personal blog may result in administrative actions being initiated against the employee. 
* No personal blogging is to be done during business hours
* [Organization] may request that the employee temporarily confine their blog commentary to topics unrelated to business (or, in rare cases, that the employee temporarily suspend their blog activity altogether) if it believes this is necessary or advisable to ensure compliance with securities regulations or other laws

### Authorized Company Representatives on Social Media
* Designated individuals in the marketing department are authorized to represent and to post on behalf of [Organization] on social media adn will be trained by the Marketing Officer regarding acceptable use of social media. 
### Acceptable Topics for Social Media
* Company marketing or promotional activity
* Upcoming corporate events
* Upcoming community events
* Company announcements
* Public interest topics
* Other topics deemed relevant or acceptable by the marketing department

The Marketing Officer shall have discretion regarding the content of official posts on social media. Topics that may be considered offensive, controversial, or in poor taste will not be posted on social media.

### Confidential Information
Confidential or sensitive customer information will not be posted by the company on any social media site. If a customer or a member of the public posts confidential or sensitive information on an official company social media site, then the marketing department will remove such information upon discovery. Members of the public will be warned not to post confidential or sensitive information on the company's social media.

### Monitoring and Response
A member of the Marketing Department will monitor the official company social media sites on a daily basis. Comments or questions by the public on official social media sites that require a response will be responded to by an authorized representative in a prompt and professional manner. Harmful or damaging statements about the company on non-company social media sites will, upon discovery, be addressed by an authorized representative in a prompt, truthful, and professional manner.
